ABOUT THE DESTINATION

Where Colonial History Meets the Indian Ocean

Perched on a rocky headland where the Indian Ocean stretches to the horizon, Galle Fort is Sri Lanka's most evocative colonial relic. Built by the Portuguese in 1588 and expanded by the Dutch in the 17th century, this UNESCO World Heritage Site is a living, breathing town of cobblestone lanes, whitewashed churches, boutique galleries, and legendary ocean sunsets.

Beyond the fort walls, Galle offers world-class surf breaks, coral reef snorkelling, whale watching, and the charm of the surrounding south coast — making it a destination that rewards slow, curious travel as much as adventure.
