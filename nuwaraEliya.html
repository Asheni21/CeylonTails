ABOUT THE DESTINATION

Sri Lanka's Cool, Misty Little England

Perched at 1,868 metres in the heart of Sri Lanka's hill country, Nuwara Eliya is a world apart — a cool, mist-draped colonial town nicknamed "Little England" for its Tudor bungalows, manicured hedgerows, and crisp mountain air. Surrounded by endless carpets of tea, it is the beating heart of Ceylon's legendary tea industry.

From the colourful Seetha Amman Temple to the glassy Gregory Lake, the cascading Ramboda Falls and the summit of Pidurutalagala — Sri Lanka's highest peak — Nuwara Eliya rewards those who come looking for cool-weather serenity, stunning highland scenery, and the world's finest cup of tea.
