ABOUT THE DESTINATION

The Eighth Wonder of the Ancient World

Rising dramatically 200 metres above the surrounding jungle plains of north-central Sri Lanka, Sigiriya — the Lion Rock — is a 5th-century royal citadel built by King Kashyapa. A UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1982, it is considered one of the best-preserved examples of ancient urban planning in the world.

From its famous frescoed ladies to the lion-paw gateway, the mirror wall, and breathtaking summit ruins, every step of the climb rewards visitors with layers of history, artistry, and panoramic wonder that stretch to the horizon.
